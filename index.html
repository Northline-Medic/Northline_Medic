About Northline_Medic

I am Northline_Medic. A 12 year EMS veteran who left the EMS field to pursue a degree in forestry. I am currently a student enrolled in indigenous science with emphasys on forest ecology. This project is focused on documenting locally growing fruiting shrubs, trees, edible plants, and culturally significant medicinal resources in northern Minnesota.

This site is created by a first-year college student, the project combines geospatial mapping, ecological research, and community knowledge to help make information about local plant resources more accessible for education, research, and conservation efforts.

Northline_Medic recognizes the deep relationship between Ojibwe communities and the lands of northern Minnesota. The project aims to respectfully highlight culturally significant plants and traditional ecological knowledge while encouraging learning, stewardship, and appreciation for the natural resources of the region.

Resources may include GIS maps, plant information, habitat descriptions, seasonal availability, and educational materials designed to support responsible exploration of local landscapes.

Resources may include GeoPackages, shapefiles, GeoJSON files, raster data, and other GIS formats.

Cultural Respect and Knowledge Stewardship

Northline_Medic recognizes that Ojibwe plant knowledge and traditional ecological teachings are part of a living cultural tradition connected to land, community, and generations of experience.

This project is developed with respect for Ojibwe communities and acknowledges the importance of learning from Ojibwe educators, elders, cultural knowledge keepers, and tribal resources when sharing information related to culturally significant plants and medicines.

Publicly available information is intended for educational purposes and does not represent or replace Indigenous teachings, community protocols, or the guidance of Ojibwe knowledge holders. Northline_Medic encourages responsible learning, respectful relationships with the land, and support for Indigenous-led knowledge sharing.
